<p style="font-size: 18px; line-height: 1.8; color: #F4F0EB; margin: 30px 0;">
<em>Concrete Poem No. 1</em> is an exploration of language as a physical, unstable force. Eight words — fragments of thought, incomplete sentences, suspended meanings — drift across the screen without resolution or destination. They can form a sentence, but never the same one twice. Each encounter with the poem is unique: the words collide, separate, and realign into endless possible readings. The piece questions the very act of reading: when words refuse to stay still, which meaning do you choose? Drawn from the vocabulary of absence and contingency — <em>if</em>, <em>when</em>, <em>was</em>, <em>dead</em> — the poem exists in the space between language and silence, between a thought that almost forms and one that dissolves before it arrives.
</p>
